Writing

Scholarship and Public Humanities

My writing explores migration, religion, diaspora, and memory through both academic and public-facing forms. Across my work, I am interested in tracing the movement of Indian indenture from British Guiana to later Indo-Guyanese diasporic life in New York City, with attention to how communities preserve cultural memory, religious practice, and historical belonging across generations.

I aim to produce scholarship that is rigorous, accessible, and meaningful to the communities whose histories it engages. My writing includes peer-reviewed articles, book reviews, public essays, and digital projects that bridge archival research, oral history, and community-based knowledge.

Selected Areas of Writing

  • Indo-Guyanese migration and religious life
  • Indian indenture in British Guiana
  • Mandirs, devotional music, and community archives
  • Oral history, family memory, and public humanities
