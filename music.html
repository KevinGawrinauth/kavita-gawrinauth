Music

Devotional Sound, Memory, and Community

This section explores music, devotional sound, and religious memory in Indo-Guyanese life. It brings together reflections on bhajans, harmonium performance, oral tradition, sacred music, and the ways sound helps communities preserve belonging across migration and displacement.

Featured Recording

Prabhuji Sukh Ka Saagar Hai

“O Lord, You Are an Ocean of Happiness”

This devotional performance and reflection explores refuge, surrender, divine guidance, migration, memory, and resilience in Indo-Guyanese religious life.

Performed by Kavita Gawrinauth on voice and harmonium, with tabla accompaniment by Kevin Gawrinauth.

Featured Recording

Teree Karam Kahaanee

“The Story of Your Actions”

Originally sung by Manna Dey for the 1971 Hindi film Chingari, with lyrics by Sahir Ludhianvi and music by Ravi.

This devotional and philosophical song reflects on karma, conscience, repentance, spiritual accountability, and the moral consequences of one’s actions.

Performed by Kavita Gawrinauth on voice and harmonium, with tabla accompaniment by Kevin Gawrinauth.

Prabhu Kaa Dhyaan

“Meditation on the Lord”

This devotional bhajan encourages continual remembrance of God, teaching that lasting peace is found by keeping the mind fixed upon the Divine rather than the temporary concerns of the world.
