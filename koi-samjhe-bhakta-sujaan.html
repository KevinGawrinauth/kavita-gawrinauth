Devotional Music

Koi Samjhe Bhakta Sujaan

← Back to Music

Koi Samjhe Bhakta Sujaan reflects upon the many forms that divine grace can take. God’s mercy, the bhajan suggests, is not experienced only through comfort, success, or abundance. At times it may appear through difficulty, uncertainty, correction, or loss. A wise devotee learns to recognize that both joyful and painful moments can shape a person’s spiritual life. Through each change in circumstance, God remains present—guiding, protecting, and judging every action with justice.

Lyrics and Translation

Hindi Lyrics and English Translation

The translation below conveys the devotional meaning of the bhajan as it is performed. The lyrics follow the transliterated version used in this performance and may differ slightly from standardized Hindi spelling or other oral traditions.

Koi samjhe bhakta sujaan,
Prabhu nit kripaa karte hain.

The wise devotee understands that God continually showers grace upon us.

Kabhi komal haath Prabhu kaa,
Kabhi bojhal haath Prabhu kaa.
Sab bhaanti kare kalyaan,
Pag pag rakshaa karte hain.

At times, God’s hand is gentle; at other times, it may feel heavy. Yet in many different ways, the Lord works for our welfare and protects us at every step.

Kabhi dhan maayaa barsaate,
Kabhi deenataa de tarsaate.
Kabhi sukh de swarg samaan,
Kabhi dukh daarun dharte hain.

Sometimes God grants wealth and worldly abundance, while at other times we experience poverty and longing. Sometimes life brings happiness resembling heaven, and at other times we are asked to endure deep sorrow.

Kabhi gyaan Prabhuji dete,
Kabhi buddhi hee har lete.
Kabhi rakhkar moorh ajaan,
Woh khud path seedhaa karte hain.

Sometimes God grants knowledge, and at other times our understanding seems to fail us. When we do not know which direction to take, the Lord may clear the path and guide us forward.

Hai roop anek kripaa ke,
Kabhi jai, kabhi haar karaate.
Prabhu kaa nirdosh vidhaan,
Nyaaya karmon kaa karte hain.

Divine grace appears in many forms. Sometimes God grants victory, and sometimes defeat. The Lord’s order is without fault, and every action is judged according to perfect justice.

Reflection

Recognizing Grace in Difficult Moments

Koi Samjhe Bhakta Sujaan asks the devotee to reconsider what divine grace looks like. It is easy to recognize God’s mercy when life brings happiness, success, or security. It is much harder to recognize that same presence during disappointment, confusion, or loss. The bhajan does not pretend that suffering is easy. Instead, it suggests that faith sometimes requires patience when the meaning of an experience is not immediately clear.

The contrast between God’s gentle and heavy hands is especially meaningful to me. A gentle hand comforts and protects, while a heavy hand corrects, redirects, or forces us to confront something we may have avoided. Both are presented as forms of care. The song therefore reminds listeners that receiving everything we desire is not necessarily the same as receiving what will ultimately help us grow.

Its final verse also connects divine grace with karma and justice. Victory and defeat are not treated simply as signs that God favors or rejects someone. Human beings may not always understand why circumstances change, but the bhajan asks them to trust that actions still carry consequences and that divine justice extends beyond immediate appearances.

This idea also affects how I think about histories of suffering. Faith may have helped people find meaning, courage, and direction during difficult circumstances, but it should not be used to excuse the injustice imposed upon them. In the history of Indian indenture, for example, religious belief could offer strength in the face of exploitation without making that exploitation acceptable or divinely ordained. Devotion helped people live through hardship; it did not make the conditions themselves just.

When I sing Koi Samjhe Bhakta Sujaan, I hear a reminder that faith is tested not only when prayers appear to be answered, but also when life takes an unexpected direction. The song encourages humility: we do not always know whether a particular moment will become a blessing, a correction, or the beginning of another path. Sometimes grace is immediately comforting. At other times, it becomes visible only after we have continued walking.
